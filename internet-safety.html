Internet Safety & Smart Browsing

The internet is powerful, but it can also be dangerous if you are not careful. Learning internet safety helps protect your personal information and devices.


1. Use Strong Passwords

Always create strong passwords using letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid simple passwords like "123456" or "password".


2. Avoid Suspicious Links

Never click links from unknown emails, messages, or websites. They may contain viruses or phishing scams.


3. Download Files Carefully

Only download files from trusted websites. Unknown downloads can contain malware that harms your computer.


4. Check Website Security

Before entering personal information, make sure the website address starts with https://. This means the website is secure.


5. Use Antivirus Software

Antivirus software helps protect your computer from viruses, spyware, and harmful programs.
